I have never known how to suffer modestly.

Give me a wound and I will build it a cathedral.

I will gild the fracture, appoint a choir, commission frescoes of the exact moment the blade entered. I will turn private devastation into architecture and then stand beneath the dome pretending I came only to admire the ceiling.

This is not vanity.

Vanity asks to be seen.

This asks for scale.

Some losses are too immense to remain events. They require columns. Marble. Incense thick enough to obscure the original body.

So I preserve them magnificently.

Every grief receives a title.  
Every absence, a throne.  
Every ending, its own immortal procession.

And when the city gathers to witness the spectacle, they mistake the pageantry for conquest.

Perhaps it is.

There are many ways to survive destruction.

Mine has always been to make it look expensive.
